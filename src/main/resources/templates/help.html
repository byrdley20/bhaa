Dashboard
  • Navigate to the dashboard by clicking the Dashboard button in the header.
  • The dashboard shows all of the aircraft and their current hobbs, notable maintenance statuses, number of starts (if applicable) and their yearly hours. There are also buttons to access the aircraft log, status and AD compliance.
  • Maintenance tasks will show as follows:
    • Red = Overdue or due very soon
      • Less than 10 hours remaining
      • Less than 5 days remaining
    • Yellow = Due soon
      • Less than 20 hours remaining
      • Less than 15 days remaining
    • White = Not due for awhile
  • To add a maintenance entry to the dashboard, an admin will need to check the Show on Dashboard box when adding/editing a maintenance type.
  • If a daily AD compliance has not been complied with for the current day, the button text will appear in red.
  • To show the starts for an aircraft, an administrator will need to check the Track Starts box when adding/editing a model.
Squawks
  • Navigate to the squawks page by clicking the Squawks button in the header.
  • To create a new squawk,
    1. Click the Create New button
    2. Populate the information
      • The Date will default to the current date
    3. Click Save
    4. The new squawk will be added to the Open list
  • To edit a squawk in the list,
    1. Click the Edit icon next to the entry to edit
    2. Update the information
    3. Click Save
  • To delete a squawk from the list,
    1. Click the Delete icon next to the entry to delete
    2. Click OK to confirm the deletion.
  • To mark a squawk as fixed, see Squawks.
  • To see all the completed squawks, click the Completed button on the squawks page.
  • To edit, delete or un-complete a completed squawk, see Squawks.
Aircraft Log
  • Navigate to an aircraft's log page by doing one of the following:
    • Clicking the Log button under the aircraft image on the Dashboard.
    • Clicking the Log button on the Aircraft Status page.
    • Clicking the Log button on the Aircraft AD Compliance page.
  • The aircraft log page shows the daily logs for the selected aircraft.
    • One month is shown at a time.
    • If the aircraft model is selected to Track Starts, the total starts are shown.
    • The monthly hobbs total is shown at the bottom of the month.
  • To create a new log entry,
    1. Click the Create New button
    2. Populate the information
      • The Date will default to the current date
      • The Hobbs Begin will default to the latest value of Hobbs End.
    3. Click Save
  • To edit a log entry in the list,
    1. Click the Edit icon next to the entry to edit
    2. Update the information
    3. Click Save
  • To delete a log entry from the list,
    1. Click the Delete icon next to the entry to delete
    2. Click OK to confirm the deletion.
  • To view the logs for a different month, click the appropriate month button.
  • To print the month being viewed, click the Print Month button.
  • To navigate to the Aircraft Status, click the Status button.
  • To navigate to the Aircraft AD Compliance, click the AD Compliance button.
  • Also see Reset Hobbs Meter and Reset Engine Time.
Aircraft Status
  • Navigate to an aircraft's status page by doing one of the following:
    • Clicking the Status button under the aircraft image on the Dashboard.
    • Clicking the Status button on the Aircraft Log page.
    • Clicking the Status button on the Aircraft AD Compliance page.
  • The aircraft status page shows the maintenance status for the selected aircraft, divided into 3 sections:
    1. Flight Hours
      • Shows the maintenance due by flight hours
      • The entries will show as follows:
        • Red = Overdue or due very soon
          • Less than 10 hours remaining
        • Yellow = Due soon
          • Less than 20 hours remaining
        • White = Not due for awhile
      • To comply with a maintenance task, see Comply With Maintenance
    2. Months
      • Shows the maintenance due by months
      • The entries will show as follows:
        • Red = Overdue or due very soon
          • Less than 5 days remaining
        • Yellow = Due soon
          • Less than 15 days remaining
        • White = Not due for awhile
      • To comply with a maintenance task, see Comply With Maintenance
    3. ADs
      • Shows the AD Compliances
      • The daily entries will be Red if they have not been complied with today.
      • The non-daily entries will show as follows:
        • Red = Overdue or due very soon
          • Less than 10 hours remaining
        • Yellow = Due soon
          • Less than 20 hours remaining
        • White = Not due for awhile
      • To comply with a daily AD compliance,
        1. Click on the Comply With/Repair icon next to the daily entry
        2. See AD Compliance for the rest of the steps.
      • To comply with a non-daily AD compliance, see Comply With Maintenance
  • To navigate to the Aircraft Log, click the Log button.
  • To navigate to the Aircraft AD Compliance, click the AD Compliance button.
  • Also see Reset Hobbs Meter and Reset Engine Time.
AD Compliance
  • Navigate to an aircraft's AD Compliance page by doing one of the following:
    • Clicking the AD Compliance button under the aircraft image on the Dashboard.
    • Clicking the AD Compliance button on the Aircraft Log page.
    • Clicking the AD Compliance button on the Aircraft Status page.
  • The aircraft AD Compliance page shows the AD Compliances for the selected aircraft.
  • If the aircraft has multiple AD Compliances, use the buttons under the aircraft model name to toggle between the different AD Compliances.
  • To view the AD Compliance logs for a different month, click the appropriate month button.
  • To comply with an AD,
    1. Click the Comply button
    2. Populate the information
      • The Date will default to the current date
    3. Click Save
  • To navigate to the Aircraft Log, click the Log button.
  • To navigate to the Aircraft Status, click the Status button.
  • Also see Reset Hobbs Meter and Reset Engine Time.

  • To create a new entry,
    1. Click the Reset Hobbs button
    2. Populate the information
      • The Date will default to the current date
    3. Click Save
  • To edit an entry in the list,
    1. Click the Edit icon next to the entry to edit
    2. Update the information
    3. Click Save
  • To delete an entry from the list,
    1. Click the Delete icon next to the entry to delete
    2. Click OK to confirm the deletion.
  • After resetting the hobbs meter, you should start the aircraft logs back at zero(0) to reflect the new hobbs hours.
  • To navigate to the Aircraft Log, click the Log button.
  • To navigate to the Aircraft Status, click the Status button.
  • To navigate to the Aircraft AD Compliance, click the AD Compliance button.

Mechanic
  • A mechanic can do everything a non-logged in person can do, plus:
    • Repair Squawks
    • Comply with Maintenance
  • To login as a mechanic,
    1. Click the Login button in the header
    2. Select mechanic as the User
    3. Enter the password
    4. Click Login
Repair Squawks
  • Only a mechanic or an admin can repair squawks
  • To repair a squawk,
    1. On the Open tab, click on the Repair icon next to the open squawk
    2. Populate the repair information
      • The Repair Date will default to the current date
    3. Click Save
    4. The entry will move to the Completed tab
  • To edit a completed squawk,
    1. Click on the Edit icon next the entry to edit
    2. Update the information
  • To delete a completed squawk,
    1. Click the Delete icon next to the entry to delete
    2. Click OK to confirm the deletion.
  • To un-complete a completed squawk,
    1. Click on the Edit icon next the entry to edit
    2. Delete the Repair Date and Repair Notes values
    3. Click Save
    4. The entry will move back to the Open tab
Comply With Maintenance
  • Only a mechanic or an admin can comply with maintenance
  • To comply with a Maintenance task or non-daily AD Compliance,
    1. Navigate to the Aircraft Status page
    2. Click the Comply With/Repair icon next to the maintenance entry
    3. Populate the information
    4. Click Save

Aircraft
  • Navigate to the aircraft page by clicking the Aircraft tab in the header
  • To create a new entry,
    1. If the aircraft is of a new model not yet in the system, then first create the model.
    2. Click the Create New button
    3. Populate the information
    4. If the aircraft has any AD Compliances that are no longer applicable for this particular aircraft, move them to the 'Not Applicable Ad Compliances' box.
    5. If the aircraft has any maintenance items that are not applicable for this particular aircraft, move them to the 'Not Applicable Maintenance' box.
    6. Click Save
  • To edit an entry in the list,
    1. Click the Edit icon next to the entry to edit
    2. Update the information
    3. Click Save
  • To delete an entry from the list,
    1. Click the Delete icon next to the entry to delete
